    2. How to choose wine

    ・Wine's 4 tastes

    Fruity

    It is opposite to "Dry." Strongth depends on where the grape grew up.
    New World is strong, and Northern Europe is week.

    Dry

    It is often used when you describe white wine as I said in "Fruity", strongth depends on where the grape grew up.

    Acidity

    It is the most characteristic taste for wine. "Tartaric acid" tastes sharp, contained a lot in white wine, and tastes good when chilled. "Malic acid" tastes mellow, contained a lot in red wine, and tastes good when warm.

    Astringency

    Strongth depends on grape varieties and prices. If we drunk "Cabernet Sauvignon"(strong), "Merlot"(middle), and "Pinot Noir,"(week) we can understand the difference.
